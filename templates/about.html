Our Roots

An Umbrian Family Recipe

Gusto Italiano started three decades ago as a small family bakery in Perugia, Italy, famous for its hand-kneaded sourdough breads and slow-roasted coffee beans. Over the years, the family moved across oceans, bringing with them ancient sourdough starters, San Marzano secrets, and the dream of marrying Italian fine-dining with the booming third-wave specialty coffee movement.

Today, we maintain the exact same rigor. We source our organic olive oils directly from family orchards in Umbria, bake our sourdough focaccias daily, and roast our coffee beans weekly to ensure a golden crema in every cup.

Our Journey

Key Milestones

1996

The Perugia Bakery

Nonno Mario opens a traditional sourdough bakery and espresso bar in Perugia, Italy.

2008

International Acclaim

The bakery expands its menu, gaining a listing in European gourmet guides for authentic thin-crust Neapolitan pizzas.

2018

The Third Wave Fusion

Mario's children incorporate organic single-origin specialty coffee roasters, introducing nitro drafts and pistachio affogatos.

2026

Gusto Italiano Online

Launching our fully automated digital table reservation, customer loyalty rewards, and smart ordering systems.
